Artist Bio (Expanded) Jacob Kelly is a Manchester-based artist working across digital print, text, and sound. His practice centres on the exploration of irrepressible trauma, using dreamlike and often unsettling imagery to draw from the surface of the unconscious. His work navigates the space between memory, fear, and imagination, attempting to articulate a form of communication that exists beyond language. Rooted in ideas of dark ecology, Kelly approaches art as an extension of the body, and the body as an extension of the land. Transformation—human, animal, and environmental—appears throughout his work as a way of resisting fixed or binary ways of thinking. Recurring motifs of amphibious or nocturnal states suggest movement between worlds: conscious and unconscious, natural and constructed, self and other. Influenced by folklore, childhood imagery, literature, and religious texts, Kelly reappropriates familiar narratives and symbols to critique structures of power, violence, and inherited trauma. His works often appear fragmented or unstable, reflecting wider social and ecological collapse while echoing cycles of decay, regeneration, and return. Working primarily through hand-crafted digital processes, Kelly deliberately rejects AI-driven production, instead emphasising labour, repetition, and material sensitivity within a medium often considered immaterial. His prints probe the tension between technological image-making and deeply human experience, foregrounding process as a site of meaning. Alongside his visual practice, he produces ambient and experimental sound work under a pseudonym, extending his interest in atmosphere, immersion, and non-verbal communication across mediums.
