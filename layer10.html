Clouds 2025

Matte Painting

Often marking the boundary between where the sound stage ends and the escapism provided by cinema begins, matte paintings in film have always entranced me. They sit at the threshold between title sequences and fantasy space, where hand-painted lettering and painted backgrounds come to life through movement, grain, and texture.

I have long been drawn to the interplay between film grain, lens effects and post-processing, where the seams show. Often I find I'm more interested in making than I am the finished work itself. Post-production inserts in older films often appear shakier, grainier, and more unstable than the footage. Title cards, special effects, and painted elements were exposed more frequently in order to merge them into one continuous product, often making something hand-painted appear totally real, in a way that feels largely absent from much contemporary filmmaking.

Inspired in part by the Columbia Pictures logo, this short animated film was made as part of that lifelong investigation. Working without photo reference, I built the image through layers of opacity, Gaussian blur, and blending modes, gradually accumulating thin, almost invisible layers of paint-like information.

Clouds seem an ideal subject for this process. Clouds are almost an illusion, created by the density of water particles suspended in the air, such that, from one angle, a cloud could appear thick and dense, and from another, thin and transparent. The clouds occlude the sun, casting shadows into themselves, creating doubles, creating optical illusions of depth and holographic effects, where the self-similar and fractal structures that repeat throughout nature become opalescent and shimmering, in this interplay of shadow and light that plays out.

This work sets out to investigate some of these effects as they seem fundamental to the techniques I use in Photoshop, where simple transformations in my work are able to create complex and naturalistic effects.
